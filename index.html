Mission Statement

To develop and apply new Cardiac MR techniques to clinical cardiovascular imaging. To educate and train researchers and clinicians in the field of CMR imaging.

Who We Are

The Cardiac MR Center (CMR) was established in 1995 as the second such CMR Center in the United States. A joint effort between the Departments of Medicine (Cardiovascular Division) and Radiology, the group is comprised of Harvard Medical School faculty, clinical fellows, research fellows, MR technologists, research support staff, and students.

What We Do

The CMR Center is dedicated to the development and clinical application of CMR applications to the cardiovascular system. These include the use of CMR for the assessment of biventricular function/volumes/mass, valvular function, myocardial viability/infarction, pericardial disease, coronary artery integrity, and the detection of subclinical atherosclerosis. Our research focuses on technical advances through advanced motion correction, image reconstruction, and contrast enhancement applied to clinical techniques including coronary artery and vein MRI, atherosclerosis imaging, MR angiography, and cardiac electrophysiology.
